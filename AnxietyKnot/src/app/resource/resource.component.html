What are cognitive distortions?

Cognitive Distortion Definition
ALL OR NOTHING THINKING Leads you to perceive things at the extremes by removing the middle ground or room for mistakes.
BLAMING OTHERS Involves holding other people antirely accountable for a negative outcome.
CATASTROPHIZING Thinking about disatrous possibilties based on a relatively small observation or event; it can lead to believing that the worst case scenario is the one that will play out.
EMOTIONAL REASONING "If I feel that way, it must be true"
FORTUNE TELLING Refers to making dramatic predictions about the future with little or no evidence.
LABELING Involves assigning negative global judgment amount of evidence based on a small amount of evidence.
MAGNIFYING THE NEGATIVE Filtering or tunnel vision, magnifying the negative fixates our thoughts on only the negative parts of a situation.
MIND READING Making assumptions about what others a thinking and feeling based on limited evidence.
MINIMIZING THE POSITIVE Actively reduces the volume of anything good.
OVERGENERALIZATION You draw broad conclusions based on just one piece of evidence.
SELF-BLAMING Involves believing that you are entirely responsible for a negative sitatuion, even for factors that are outside of your control.
SHOULD STATEMENTS Involve creating narrow and inflexible rules abouw how you and other people should behave.
